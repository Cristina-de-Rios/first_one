Accurate craftsmanship and high-quality materials

Our shoes are made with great attention to detail and refined materials. If properly maintained they will get better with time. Full-grain leather will develop its unique natural patina as it ages. Our leather soles are easily resolable thanks to theGoodyear and Blake constructions.

Goodyear Welt Construction

Goodyear welted shoes feature a leather strip (welt) attached through the upper and insole. A waxed thread is then sewn to hold the materials firmly together. It’s a strong, durable and breathable construction. Plus it has a cork filling that molds to your foot-bed over time, as a result, after a few wears the shoes get so comfortable they feel custom-like.


Blake Construction

Blake constructed shoes are created by stitching together the upper, insole and outsole. This method allows for a lightweight and flexible structure that’s extremely durable. Since the welt has no structural function in this construction, the shoes can be made with sleeker profiles and close-cut soles providing a more elegant look.

How should I take care of my new shoes?

Taking good care of your shoes is a no brainer, they´ll last longer and you’ll look sharper.

For smooth leather, brush well once a week to remove any dust and dirt. You can wet the brush to clean the welt if needed. Since leather is a natural material it can dry out, just like your skin. To prevent this, use a cream-based conditioner once in a while, apply a small amount with a damp cloth and rub in circles. This is virtuous enough; you should be admired if you make this a ritual. Anyway, if you want to go further you can use a neutral polish and finish with a polish brush if some extra shine is desired.

For suede or nubuck leather, the best thing you can do is to spray them generously with a protective spray before the first wear. This will repel water and prevent dirt stains over time; Repeat at least twice a month. For heavy stains, rub with a suede/nubuck cleaning gum and then raise the nap with a brush. If you feel like really going the extra mile, you can also raise the nap holding your shoes over a steaming pot of water.

Are shoe-trees really necessary?
They are not strictly necessary but can be helpful in several ways. The use of shoetrees prevents deep creases and will absorb humidity. This eliminates odors and make the original shape of the shoes last longer. Plus, some women have reported that it’s a turn on when they see such an act of manliness.
